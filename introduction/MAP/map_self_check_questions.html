MAP adds a prior over parameters. It maximizes likelihood times prior, or equivalently log-likelihood plus log-prior.
When the prior is uniform, very weak, or dominated by a large amount of data.
The negative log of a zero-mean Gaussian prior is proportional to the squared L2 norm of the parameters.
For an interior mode, it is (k + alpha - 1) / (n + alpha + beta - 2).
MAP returns one point estimate. Full Bayesian inference keeps the entire posterior distribution and therefore preserves uncertainty.
