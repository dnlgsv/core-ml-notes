MAP estimates parameters by maximizing the posterior, which is proportional to likelihood times prior.
The regularization penalty is the negative log-prior. L2 corresponds to a Gaussian prior and L1 corresponds to a Laplace prior.
MLE can overreact to a small sample. MAP uses the prior to stabilize the estimate and avoid extreme values when evidence is weak.
